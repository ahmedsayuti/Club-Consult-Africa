Latif Amadou Signs for Saudi Arabian Giants Al Ansar Club

Ghanaian striker Latif Amadou completes move to Saudi Arabia Premier League side Al Ansar Club on a six-month deal, aiming to boost their domestic dominance.

January 18, 2026 7 min read By Kwame Asante
Back to All Articles

Latif Amadou joins Al Ansar Club for the remainder of the 2025/26 season

Transfer Completed: Amadou Moves to Al Ansar

Saudi Arabia Premier League side, Al Ansar Club have secured the services of Ghana striker Latif Amadou on a six-month deal. The 26-year-old has now completed his transfer to the Arabian giants after a successful medical and agreeing to personal terms with the Madina-based club.

"I'm very happy because they (Al Ansar Club) have shown me trust and given me lots of confidence. So I will give my all to score goals to help them promote to the top league in shaa Allah."

- Latif Amadou

Second Saudi Arabian Stint

Amadou had sealed an initial move to Saudi Arabia with Al-Khaleej, but parted ways with the Arabian club after just four months. This move to Al Ansar Club represents a fresh start and an opportunity to establish himself in the competitive Saudi Arabian football landscape.

"I learned a lot during my time with Al-Khaleej," Amadou stated. "The Saudi Arabian league is competitive and physical. Now with Al Ansar, I have the chance to apply those lessons and make a real impact."

Latif Amadou - Player Profile

Position: Striker
Age: 26 years
Nationality: Ghanaian
Previous Club: Al-Khaleej (Saudi Arabia)
Contract Duration: 6 months
New Club: Al Ansar Club

Career Journey: Ghana to the Gulf

Latif Amadou's football journey began in Ghana where he developed his skills in local academies before catching the attention of scouts with his prolific scoring ability. His move to Saudi Arabia represents his first major international transfer and an opportunity to showcase his talents in Asia's growing football market.

His career progression includes:

  • Ghana Youth Development: Honed his skills in several Ghanaian youth academies, known for his clinical finishing and aerial ability
  • Local League Experience: Featured for several Ghana Premier League clubs, developing his physical game and tactical understanding
  • Al-Khaleej (Saudi Arabia): First international experience, adapting to Middle Eastern football culture and style
  • Al Ansar Club: New chapter with increased expectations and a platform to establish himself as a key player

Latif Amadou during his official presentation at Al Ansar Club

What Amadou Brings to Al Ansar Club

Club officials have highlighted several key attributes that made Amadou an attractive signing:

Attacking Qualities

  • Clinical finishing with both feet in the penalty area
  • Excellent aerial ability and heading accuracy
  • Strong hold-up play and ability to bring teammates into attack
  • Intelligent movement off the ball to create space

Tactical Versatility

  • Can play as a lone striker or in a two-striker system
  • Understanding of defensive responsibilities from the front
  • Experience in different tactical setups and formations
  • Ability to press high or play as a target man

Physical Attributes

  • Strong physical presence to compete with defenders
  • Good stamina for high-intensity matches in Saudi conditions
  • Acceleration over short distances to get behind defenses
  • Balance and strength to shield the ball under pressure

Saudi Arabian Football Landscape

The Saudi Arabian Premier League has been growing in stature and competitiveness in recent years, with increased investment in both local and international talent. Al Ansar Club's acquisition of Latif Amadou represents their commitment to strengthening their squad with quality African talent.

Amadou's arrival will boost the ranks of Al Ansar as they bid to extend their domestic dominance in Saudi Arabia. The club has shown ambition in the transfer market and sees the Ghanaian striker as a key component in their attacking options for the remainder of the season.

Future Prospects and International Ambitions

At 26, Latif Amadou is entering what should be the prime years of a striker's career. His move to Al Ansar Club represents an opportunity to establish himself in a competitive league and potentially earn a longer-term contract based on his performances.

While consistent performances in Saudi Arabia may not directly lead to immediate national team call-ups for Ghana, strong goal-scoring form could put him on the radar of Black Stars selectors, especially with the upcoming African competitions.

Conclusion: Latif Amadou's transfer to Al Ansar Club represents a significant opportunity for the Ghanaian striker to revive his international career and establish himself in Saudi Arabian football. With his technical skills, physical attributes, and determination to succeed, he's well-positioned to make a positive impact.
