Kwame Bonsu Wins Tunisian Premier League with Espérance

Ghanaian midfielder Kwame Bonsu celebrates his first Tunisian league title as Espérance Tunis secure their 30th championship with three games to spare, remaining unbeaten this season.

April 12, 2026 5 min read By Kwame Asante
Back to All Articles

Kwame Bonsu celebrates winning the Tunisian Premier League with Espérance Tunis

League Title Secured

Kwame Bonsu has won the Tunisian league after his side Espérance Tunis secured their 30th league title on Sunday.

Espérance drew 1-1 with SC Chebba, a result which was enough to ensure that the Moïn Chaâbani’s men wrap up the league title with three games to go.

"This is an incredible achievement for me and the team. Winning the league in my first season with Espérance is something special."

Kwame Bonsu - Title Achievement

Player: Kwame Bonsu
Nationality: Ghanaian
Club: Espérance Tunis
Title: Tunisian Premier League
Club's Titles: 30th league title
Games Remaining: 3 games to spare
Season Record: Unbeaten in league
Goal Difference: +23

Match Contribution

Bonsu played in the first half of the game and was substituted in the second half as he enjoyed his first league success with his new club.

Ghanaian Legacy in Tunisia

He has now became the fifth Ghanaian player to win the Tunisian league, after Harrison Afful won the league four times with Espérance, Seidu Salifu won the league in 2014/15 with Club Africain, Moussa Narry and Sadat Bukari lifted the Tunisian league trophy with Étoile Sportive du Sahel in 2006/07.

Season Dominance

Espérance are still unbeaten in the league this season and have a nine point lead plus a healthy goal difference of +23.
